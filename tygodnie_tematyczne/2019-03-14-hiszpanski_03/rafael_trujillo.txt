'''Rafael Joaquín Trujillo Villar''' (born 14 December 1975 in [[La Línea de la Concepción]], [[Cádiz]])<ref>[http://www.finnclass.org/media-finn/sailor-profiles/369-rafa-trujillo Sailor profiles Rafael Trujillo, ESP]</ref> is a Spanish [[sailboat racing|sailor]] of the [[Finn (dinghy)|Finn class]] and Olympic medalist.<ref>[https://members.sailing.org/biog.php?unique=1473218977.5063&id=ESPRT7&js=1 ISAF profile]</ref> He won the [[Finn Gold Cup]] once and competed in four straight [[Olympic Games]], winning a silver medal in [[2004 Summer Olympics|Athens  2004]].<ref name=sref>{{cite Sports-Reference |title=Rafael Trujillo |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/tr/rafael-trujillo-1.html |accessdate=25 March 2011}}</ref> 

He sailed with [[+39 Challenge]] in the [[2007 Louis Vuitton Cup]].

Trujillo was also part of the ''[[Mapfre (yacht)|Mapfre]]'' yacht in the [[2014–15 Volvo Ocean Race]],<ref>[http://www.volvooceanrace.com/en/people/397_Rafael-Trujillo-Villar.html Rafael Trujillo Villar]</ref>  and coaches 2013 world champion [[Jorge Zarif]].<ref>[http://www.finnwelle.de/home/regatten-termine/2016/masters-2016/ Rafael Trujillo wins first Finn World Masters in Torbole]</ref>
