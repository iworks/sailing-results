'''Sir Charles Benedict Ainslie''', [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]] (born 5 February 1977) is an English [[sailing (sport)|competitive sailor]].

Ainslie is one of the most successful sailors in Olympic history. He won medals at five consecutive Olympics from 1996 onwards, including gold at the four Games held between 2000 and 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wikiwaparz.com/foreign-musics/ainslie-grow/"Ainslie's|title=ambition: Sailing hero takes on America's Cup challenge"|publisher=}}</ref><ref name=Olympics />

He is one of three athletes to win medals in five different Olympic Games in sailing, being the third person to win five Olympic medals in that sport (after [[Torben Grael]] and [[Robert Scheidt]]) and also the second to win four gold medals, after [[Paul Elvstrøm]].<ref name=Olympics>{{cite news |first=Steven |last=Morris |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/aug/05/ben-ainslie-olympic-gold-great-britain |title=Ben Ainslie wins his fourth Olympic gold medal for Great Britain |date=5 August 2012 |work=[[The Guardian]] |accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/olympics/article-2183974/London-2012-Olympics-Sailing--Ben-Ainslie-wins-gold.html |title=London 2012 Olympics: Sailing – Ben Ainslie wins gold |last=McEvoy |first=Jonathan |date=5 August 2012 |work=[[Mail Online]] |accessdate=5 August 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/olympics/18907123 |title=Ben Ainslie wins fourth Olympics sailing gold medal |accessdate=5 August 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120805190425/http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/olympics/18907123 |archive-date=5 August 2012 |dead-url=yes |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Early life==
Ainslie was born in [[Macclesfield]], England to Roderick and Sue Ainslie. Roderick captained a boat that took part in the first [[Whitbread Round The World Race]] in 1973.<ref name=20Things /> Ben's elder sister, Fleur, is married to Jerome Pels, secretary general of the [[International Sailing Federation]] (ISAF).<ref name=20Things /> Ainslie was educated at two [[independent school]]s: at the [[Terra Nova School]] in a rural area near the village of [[Holmes Chapel]] in [[Cheshire]] in north west England, and [[Truro School]] in the city of [[Truro]], Cornwall, followed by [[Peter Symonds College|Peter Symonds Sixth Form]], [[Winchester]], [[Hampshire]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ben Ainslie profile|work=Sailor Biography |publisher=ISAF UK |url=http://www.sailing.org/bio.asp?ID=GBRCA1 |accessdate=17 August 2008}}</ref>

==Career==
Ainslie learned to sail at [[Restronguet Creek]] near [[Falmouth, Cornwall]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/may/18/ben-ainslie-olympic-torch-london?intcmp=239 |title=Ben Ainslie's Olympic torch run sets course for London Games |date=18 May 2012 |publisher=The Guardian |author=Steven Morris}}</ref>

He started sailing at the age of eight and first competed at the age of ten.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.benainslie.com/about |title=About Ben |work=www.benainslie.com |accessdate=6 November 2011}}</ref> His first international competition was aged twelve at the 1989 [[Optimist (dinghy)|Optimist]] world championships held in Japan where he placed 73rd. {{citation needed|date=April 2014}}

===Olympics===
Ainslie won silver at the [[1996 Olympic Games]] and gold in the [[Sailing at the 2000 Summer Olympics|2000 Summer Olympics]] in the [[Laser (dinghy)|Laser]] class.  He gained some {{convert|18|kg|lb stlb}} and moved to the larger [[Finn (dinghy)|Finn]] class for the [[Sailing at the 2004 Summer Olympics|2004 Summer Olympics]], where he won gold, a feat he repeated in the [[Sailing at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Finn class|2008]] and [[Sailing at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Finn class|2012]] competitions. [http://www.nmmc.co.uk/index.php?/collections/featured_boats/ben_ainslies_finn Both his gold medal winning Laser and Finn dinghies are currently displayed at the National Maritime Museum Cornwall].

On 19 May 2012, Ainslie became the first person to carry the Olympic torch in the UK. Starting the 70-day tour of the United Kingdom at [[Land's End]], he was the first of  8,000 torch carriers.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-18119893 |title=Ben Ainslie Starts the Torch Relay |date=19 May 2012 |work=[[The BBC]] |accessdate=19 May 2012}}</ref>  He was selected on 11 August 2012 to carry the flag for the Great Britain team at the [[2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony flag bearers|London 2012 Olympics closing ceremony]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/olympics/19226365 |title=Ben Ainslie to carry British flag at Olympics closing ceremony |publisher=[[BBC Sport]] |date=10 August 2012 |accessdate=10 August 2012}}</ref>

===America's Cup===
[[File:Sir Charles Benedict Ainslie with fans in 2014.jpg|thumb|300px|right|Sir Ben Ainslie with fans in 2014]]
* One World Challenge – Seattle Yacht Club<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scoop.co.nz/stories/CU0406/S00091.htm|title=Grant Dalton introduces the team - Scoop News|first1=|last1=Monday|first2=14|last2=June 2004|first3=4:51 pm Press Release: Emirates Team New|last3=Zeal|website=www.scoop.co.nz}}</ref>
* [[Team New Zealand]] – [[Royal New Zealand Yacht Squadron]]
At the beginning of 2005 Ainslie worked in the role of [[:wikt:tactician|tactician]] in the New Zealand-based [[Team New Zealand]] who were preparing to compete in the challenger selection process to win the right to Challenge for the 32nd [[America's Cup]]. In May 2005 Ainslie took the decision to relinquish the role of tactician and focus on improving his [[match racing]] skills as helmsman of the 'B' boat used to practise and tune the race boat helmed by [[Dean Barker (yachtsman)|Dean Barker]].
* [[Team Origin]] – [[Royal Thames Yacht Club]]
Ainslie figureheaded a British challenge for the Cup alongside [[Sir Keith Mills]] the team was named [[Team Origin]]. However this challenge withdrew without competing following a period of discussion regarding the future format of the event.
* [[Oracle Team USA]] – [[Golden Gate Yacht Club]]
For the [[2013 America's Cup]], Ainslie was recruited as a tactician by Oracle Team USA, as a replacement for John Kostecki during an Oracle practice session on 11 September 2013.<ref>{{cite web |author=Ben Ainslie |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/americas-cup/9156628/Ainslie-in-tacticians-role-for-practice-session |title=Ainslie in tactician's role for practice session |publisher=Stuff.co.nz |accessdate=11 September 2013}}</ref> On 12 September, the following day, he replaced Kostecki going into race six of the [[2013 America's Cup]].<ref>[http://news.sky.com/story/1146136/ben-ainslie-steers-americas-cup-comeback "Ben Ainslie Steers America's Cup Comeback"], Sky.com; retrieved 24 September 2013.</ref> His Oracle Team USA beat Team New Zealand in the America's Cup decider in San Francisco on 25 September.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/sailing/24274103 |title=Ben Ainslie's USA beat Team New Zealand in decider |date=26 September 2013 |work=BBC Sport |accessdate=26 September 2013}}</ref>
* [[Land Rover BAR|Ben Ainslie Racing]] – [[Royal Yacht Squadron]] Racing Ltd
In January 2012, Ben Ainslie announced the formation of a team to compete in the America's Cup, called Ben Ainslie Racing (BAR). His team competed in the AC45 class of the [[2011–13 America's Cup World Series]],<ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/general/sailing/ainslie-defends-oracle-cup-role-6287738.html Ainslie defends Oracle cup role], Independent, 11 January 2012. Retrieved 30 July 2016.</ref> [[2014 Extreme Sailing Series]] and set a multihull record for the [[Round the Island Race]]. The team won the [[2015–16 America's Cup World Series]] and is preparing for the [[2017 America's Cup]].

In February 2017 Ainslie talked about the America's Cup and looked back on his Olympic success, "It hasn’t all been glory..There have been a few bumps down the road. But yes, I’m not very good at not achieving the goals I set out."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sailing/2017/02/08/sir-ben-ainslie-makes-plans-americas-cup-glory-paradise-island/|title=Sir Ben Ainslie makes plans for America's Cup glory on paradise island|publisher=}}</ref>

===World Match Racing Tour===
In December 2010, Ainslie finished in first place in the [[World Match Racing Tour]], and is the 2010 ISAF Match Racing Champion. {{citation needed|date=April 2014}}

===The Extreme Sailing Series===
In January 2014, it was announced that Ainslie would compete in the 2014 Extreme Sailing Series as part of his preparation for the America's Cup. The eight-race event will see him compete in a {{convert|40|ft}} multi-hull boat.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/sailing/25659753|title=Sir Ben Ainslie to compete in Extreme Sailing Series|date=8 January 2014|publisher=|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ainslie lives in [[Lymington]], and belongs to the Royal Lymington Yacht Club. He is coached by David Howlett, who raced a [[Finn (dinghy)|Finn dinghy]] at the [[1976 Summer Olympics]]. He supports [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]].<ref name=20Things>{{cite web |url=http://www.yachtingworld.com/olympics-2012/beach/532360/20-things-about-ben-ainslie |title=20 things about Ben Ainslie |author=Elaine Bunting |publisher=yachtingworld.com |date=5 August 2012 |accessdate=10 August 2012}}</ref>

In August 2014, Ainslie was one of 200 public figures who were signatories to a letter to ''[[The Guardian]]'' expressing their hope that Scotland would vote to remain part of the United Kingdom in September's [[Scottish independence referendum, 2014|referendum on that issue]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2014/aug/07/celebrities-open-letter-scotland-independence-full-text |title=Celebrities' open letter to Scotland – full text and list of signatories &#124; Politics |publisher=theguardian.com |date=2014-08-07 |accessdate=2014-08-26}}</ref>

On 20 December 2014, Ainslie married former Sky Sports News presenter [[Georgie Thompson]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ybw.com/news/sailing/537648/sir-ben-ainslie-marries-georgie-thompson-at-hampton-court-palace |title=Ben Ainslie Marries |date=23 Dec 2014 |website=YBW |accessdate=3 Jan 2015}}</ref> The couple's first child, Bellatrix, was born on 16 July 2016.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sailingscuttlebutt.com/2016/07/26/always-sunny-ainslie-land | title=Always Sunny in Ainslie Land | date=26 July 2016 | access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | author=Emily Sheridan | title='Our little star has arrived': Sir Ben Ainslie and wife Georgie welcome their first child Bellatrix | url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-3701159/Sir-Ben-Ainslie-wife-Georgie-welcome-child-Bellatrix.html | work=[[Daily Mail]] | date=21 July 2016 | access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref>
